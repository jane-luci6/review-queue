# LUCI Website Strategy — Talk Track

**Audience:** Mike, Nick  
**Companion doc:** `website-strategy-brief.html`  
**Format:** 5–6 slides, visual design TBD. Headline + speaker bullets + ask per slide.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Headline:** LUCI website strategy: purpose before pages

**Speaker bullets:**
- We are refreshing lucisystems.com to tell one product story — not two nav pillars.
- This meeting aligns on *why* the site exists and what success looks like.
- IA, wireframe, and build details are Phase 2 — after we agree here.

**Ask:** Does this framing — strategy first, pages second — work for how you want to review this?

---

## Slide 2 — The problem

**Headline:** Complex category; buyers won't hunt menus; AI changed discovery

**Speaker bullets:**
- Enterprise buyers research in AI tools first — they expect answers, not menu archaeology.
- LUCI serves six functions across six industries with a deep Platform story; simple nav alone forces a tradeoff.
- Competitors still ship brochure sites; agentic discoverability is an emerging advantage.

**Ask:** Do you see the same shift in how prospects arrive and what they expect on first visit?

---

## Slide 3 — What the site must do

**Headline:** Pipeline + category authority — pass the THE THING test

**Speaker bullets:**
- **Primary job:** curiosity → conviction → conversation (demo/contact).
- **Secondary job:** LUCI is *the* multimedia orchestration engine — category definition, not feature sprawl.
- **THE THING test:** a CIO leaves knowing what LUCI is and why it matters in 30 seconds.
- **Not the job:** replace sales, expose The Signal publicly, or split into two product nav pillars.

**Ask:** Are these the right two jobs? Anything missing or overweighted?

---

## Slide 4 — How we'll win

**Headline:** Minimal nav + deep Platform story + Navless intent layer

**Speaker bullets:**
- **Static nav stays simple:** Home · Platform · Industries · Who we serve · Resources · Company.
- **Platform page** carries software + embedded partnership in one scroll — not two destinations.
- **Navless (recommended):** one intelligent front door — intent Q&A, role-based playlists, AI discoverability — on top of the site, not instead of it.
- **Guardrails:** on-brand, one entry point, public content only; Signal stays out of the AI layer.

**Ask:** Comfortable recommending Navless as the experience layer, with a pilot after core pages exist?

---

## Slide 5 — Proof & conversion

**Headline:** Resources for proof; demo CTA; Signal gated

**Speaker bullets:**
- Proof escalates: homepage teaser → Platform depth → case studies under Resources.
- Portfolio line OK; stat+logo combos never in brand copy.
- Primary CTA: Talk to us / Demo — consistent, no popups or clutter.
- The Signal: customer login only (footer/utility) — not in public nav or homepage promo.

**Ask:** Any concerns on proof placement or keeping Signal fully customer-gated?

---

## Slide 6 — Decisions today

**Headline:** Message lockup · Navless pilot · Phase 2 IA

**Speaker bullets:**
1. Approve strategic frame (one product, dual website job).
2. Approve **proposed** tagline/sub-tagline — or redirect:
   - *The Orchestration Engine for Enterprise Multimedia*
   - *One interface to control, automate, and execute the entire guest experience.*
3. Approve Navless as recommended intelligent layer (pilot scope + budget conversation).
4. Green-light Phase 2: IA wireframe walkthrough.
5. Confirm The Signal stays customer-only.

**Ask:** Which of these can we lock today? What needs more discussion before Phase 2?
