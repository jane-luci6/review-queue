Executive summary

Strategic foundation: Minimal canonical site + intelligent discovery layer + AISEO infrastructure + interactive proof.

This is where B2B discovery and evaluation are heading. Buyers research in AI tools before they visit; buying committees self-serve across six or more functions; and the sites that win treat the website as infrastructure for qualified pipeline — not a brochure that hopes sales fills the gaps. For LUCI, that model is the best path to capture qualified leads and position as category leader: show authority before the first conversation, and make every stakeholder in the room find the proof they need without a rep in the loop.

  • Minimal canonical site. A stable IA — Home · Platform · Industries · By function · Resources · Company — carries the structured story human visitors and search engines can rely on.

  • Intelligent discovery layer. Plain-language intent entry on top of the static site — conversational Q&A, role-based proof paths, shareable playlists — so buyers state what they need instead of hunting menus.

  • AISEO infrastructure. llms.txt, semantic HTML, and structured schema so LUCI is discoverable and citable when buyers research via ChatGPT, Perplexity, and similar tools — before they ever click through.

  • Interactive proof. Platform visuals, in-action video, and explorable demos that show what LUCI actually does — especially in the hero — so visitors immediately understand this is property-wide orchestration, not content production or equipment sales.

This refresh positions lucisystems.com as the public face of LUCI’s category leadership — an advanced platform with decades of operational expertise built in as services.

Objectives

  • Own the category. Establish LUCI as the resource for enterprise property multimedia — platform plus decades of operational expertise built in. Authority is shown through proof and depth, not proclaimed in hero copy.

  • Clarify the value prop. Roll out updated tagline, sub-tagline, and boilerplate — a consistent hierarchy of messages that tells buyers what LUCI is, how it works, and why it matters.

  • Present the full offering. Platform and embedded operations carry equal weight. The Systems team is a core differentiator the current site underplays.

  • Show industry relevance. Make clear where LUCI makes a difference across target verticals.

  • Earn qualified pipeline. When visitors understand what LUCI is and whether it fits, qualified conversations follow.

Discovery layer — build vs. buy: Navless is one embeddable option (content ingestion, conversational guides, playlists, AISEO, CRM export). LUCI could also build a comparable layer in-house. Vendor vs. custom is a Phase 3 decision; the strategic requirement is the capability.

Why now

A major LUCI platform release ships in August; expansion into new industries follows in the coming months. The site refresh establishes LUCI’s positioning, industry authority, and a technology story that shows the platform is years ahead of conventional property multimedia — not keeping pace with the market, but setting where it’s headed.

Structural changes

Starting point: the published site at lucisystems.com is a single scrolling page — no global navigation. Content flows in one vertical story: hero, what we do, logos, capabilities, case studies, thought partners, impact stats, contact. The executive team has not seen later IA explorations; this is what we are changing from.

The four-part foundation means evolving that monolith into a minimal multi-page site — adding nav for the first time, in a restrained way, so buyers can self-serve by topic without losing the clarity of a focused story.

Navigation — add, minimally

Launch adds a slim global nav where none exists today:

Home · Platform · Industries · By function · Resources · Company — plus persistent Talk to us / Demo. No dropdowns at launch.

  • Platform — depth the single page cannot carry: how LUCI works, structural differentiation, and embedded partnership (“How we work with you”) in one destination.

  • By function — one page for leadership, operations, technology, finance, marketing, and facilities/AV; not six nav items or microsites.

  • Industries — vertical relevance; launch with proof-gated verticals only.

  • Resources — proof hub for case studies and content currently embedded mid-scroll.

  • Intent entry — discovery layer or utility trigger, outside the nav.

What moves out of the single-page scroll

  • “What we do” + capabilities — become homepage teaser + Platform depth (variable reduction, disk model, partnership).

  • Case studies — move to Resources; homepage keeps one proof teaser with link.

  • Thought partners / team story — fold into Platform (embedded operations); not a separate public destination.

  • Impact stat blocks — rework or retire per brand rules; proof lives in case studies and visuals, not spreadsheet-style grids.

  • Contact — persistent CTA across pages, not only a form at the bottom of a long scroll.

  • The Signal — customer login/utility only; not in public nav.

Internal wireframes explored heavier nav patterns; those are planning artifacts only. Phase 2 aligns the build doc to this direction — website-ia-wireframe.html + website strategy (outline).

Content & proof

Structure sets the paths; content and proof close the belief gap. This is the highest-priority production work after IA approval.

Hero and header — show what LUCI is

LUCI is easy to misread — multimedia content, media equipment, signage vendor, AV integrator. Copy alone cannot fix that at executive speed. The hero must show the platform in action: property-wide orchestration from a single interface — real UI, real property context, or a compelling in-action visual (disk model, zone map, interface commanding multiple screens). The visitor should grasp what they are buying in seconds, before they read a paragraph.

Interactive and in-action proof

  • Platform demos and walkthroughs — short product-in-action video, clickable tours, or explorable visuals on Platform and homepage; self-serve before a sales call (Ramp-style interactive proof is the benchmark).

  • Structural visuals — disk model, rack before/after, property zone diagrams; make the abstract concrete.

  • Role- and industry-specific proof — case studies, clips, and outcomes segmented so each function sees evidence that matches their concern.

  • Operational language — copy stays practical and validatable; broad claims without backing read like every other vendor.

  • Escalating depth — homepage teaser → Platform depth → Resources proof; each level answers the question the last one raised.

  • Portfolio that confirms the copy — logos and case studies used strategically, not promotionally.

Audiences & what they must leave believing

Six buyer functions (from the Persona Guide). Each should leave with one clear belief.

  • Leadership (CEO / GM)

    The floor already shapes guest behavior — LUCI makes that influence deliberate and property-wide, so guest experience works as a lever for revenue and brand, not infrastructure nobody runs with intent.

  • Technology (CIO / IT)

    Enterprise AV can run as standard network infrastructure — one interface across the property, without proprietary hardware sprawl or specialist programming lock-in.

  • Operations (COO / gaming ops)

    The property can run tighter with one accountable team — routine work automated, the unexpected handled fast, end to end.

  • Finance (CFO)

    Multimedia infrastructure can appreciate instead of depreciate — one predictable subscription replaces the five-to-seven-year refresh cycle, and capability grows on the same spend.

  • Marketing / Guest experience (CMO)

    Campaigns and guest messaging aren't limited to the screens marketing already controls — LUCI lets them schedule and coordinate ads, campaigns, and messaging across any screen on the property, as one program they run on their timeline, without routing every change through AV or IT.

  • Facilities / AV

    Property AV doesn't have to mean server-room runs and reactive firefighting — LUCI keeps their expertise central while shifting the job to proactive command of one coherent, property-wide system.

Role-path architecture — homepage vs. nav

Listing functions only in the nav is not enough. Most executives land on the homepage and never open a dropdown; role pages buried in menus see low traffic. The more effective model is layered:

  • Homepage — start broad (category, hero visual, what LUCI is); then a dedicated section calls out how LUCI helps Marketing, Operations, Finance, Technology, Leadership, and Facilities/AV — each row states the belief in their language and links to depth. This is the primary routing moment for first-time visitors.

  • By function — one page with full proof per buyer function; the destination homepage rows point to. Not six microsites.

  • Nav — By function as a single item signals the page exists for return visitors and deliberate explorers; it does not carry the whole job alone.

  • Discovery layer — catches buyers who state intent in plain language instead of self-selecting a role.

  • Reconnect at conversion — every path returns to the same primary CTA; different evidence, one commercial next step.

Conversion model

The site earns pipeline when visitors understand what LUCI is and whether it fits — especially executives and AV leads who want to self-serve before they talk to anyone. The primary CTA is the destination; the path to it must be quick, clear, and rich enough to build confidence without wrestling through dense technical docs.

  • Primary CTA

    Talk to us / Demo — consistent placement across key pages; the single action we optimize toward.

  • Self-serve path

    Platform depth, industry relevance, By function, and Resources (case studies, visuals) let buyers research on their own terms. Scannable structure, escalating clarity, and proof by function — so a CIO, CFO, or facilities lead can grasp what LUCI does and why it matters in minutes, not a document deep-dive. Every layer should make the demo request feel informed, not premature.

  • Intent discovery path

    Plain-language Q&A is a major conversion route — not a replacement for the static site, but the front door for buyers who state intent (“uptime proof for airports,” “what does LUCI replace in the rack?”) instead of hunting menus. An intelligent discovery layer surfaces curated answers and role-based proof paths from site content, then hands off to conversion via configured forms and schedulers, links to site CTAs, and engagement data to CRM. Conversion is intentional — those touchpoints get wired in the pilot; the layer does not auto-close deals on its own.

  • Anti-clutter

    No popups or exit-intent modals. No generic floating sales-chat widget at launch — the discovery layer is on-brand and intent-driven, not interrupt-driven qualification. A conventional chat bot may follow later; behavior-based CTA emphasis on long pages only (e.g. Platform scroll).
