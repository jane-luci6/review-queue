LUCI Systems · Field Activation Guide 02

The principle

One floor. One platform. One strategy.

Before LUCI, most casino and resort teams worked the floor separately. Marketing ran the signage, Operations shaped the gaming environment, A/V kept the screens and sound running — each in its own lane, on its own equipment, with few ways to coordinate.

Now every screen, zone, and audio channel moves on a single platform. Marketing, Gaming and Operations, A/V and Facilities, and IT aren’t working separate equipment that happens to share a building. They’re working the same floor, through the same platform, toward the same guest.

That’s the real opportunity. When your teams decide together how the floor should work — which messages run where, how energy builds through the day, what happens when a jackpot hits or an event lets out — they’re building one intentional guest-experience strategy. Set the plan together, and each team can move fast in its own lane, because everyone knows how the pieces fit. Real-time changes still happen; they just happen against a shared plan.

Plan the floor together. Then activate it.

01

Marketing · Guest Experience

Every screen on the property is now yours to program — by zone, by daypart, in real time. The reach that used to live on a handful of dedicated signs now covers the whole floor.

The right message, in the right zone, at the right moment becomes a lever you control — to extend time on device, lift on-property spend, and drive trip frequency.

  • Push a message to every screen in under two minutes.

    A promotion changes, an event is cancelled, the weather turns — you make the change yourself, from your own device, and it’s live across the floor in moments.

    • Update every screen, or just a set of zones, from your own device
    • Override scheduled content, then return to the plan
    • Changes go live immediately — no tickets, no calls, no waiting

    Try it →Find one piece of content that needed outside help to change last time. Change it yourself today.

  • Daypart your restaurant promotions to catch guests at the decision point.

    Drop a dining spot into floor programming in the hour before lunch and again before dinner — the windows when guests decide whether to stay on property — then hand back to the regular rotation.

    • Run dining spots property-wide ahead of lunch and dinner
    • Reach screens across the floor, not just the displays near the restaurants
    • Set timing and frequency once; it runs on schedule every day

    Try it →Build a pre-lunch dining spot for this week. Stand on the floor at 11am, watch it run, then hand back on its own.

  • Run different content in every zone — indoor and outdoor — from one control panel.

    The sports book, the main floor, the bar, and the marquee can all show different things at once. Every display, including external signage connected through LUCI, is programmed from the same place.

    • Assign zone-specific schedules independently of each other
    • Bring the marquee and outdoor signs under the same control as indoor screens
    • Add a promotional push in one zone without touching the rest
    • Keep messaging consistent from the highway to the floor

    Try it →Set up zone-specific content for your next promotional weekend, and confirm which external displays are in the system.

  • Build an event campaign that escalates automatically.

    Schedule a rotation that grows an event’s presence — more zones, more frequency — as the date approaches, and that puts your slow dayparts to work in between. Build it once and it runs.

    • Start in one zone two weeks out; expand to the full floor the week of
    • Fill historically slow dayparts with loyalty, dining, and entertainment offers
    • Return every zone to normal content automatically after the date

    Try it →Map your next major event and build a countdown schedule that ramps on its own.
