HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Pull from this when you’re writing anything external — emails, decks, proposals, LinkedIn posts, event copy. The tagline and value prop are exact; use them as written. For the deeper philosophy behind this messaging, see the LUCI Philosophy document.

TAGLINE

The Orchestration Engine for Enterprise Multimedia
One interface to control, automate, and execute the entire guest experience.

VALUE PROPOSITION & BOILERPLATE

Use this as standard language for emails, decks, and proposals.

LUCI Systems orchestrates every layer of technology running your property — AV, signage, building, and operational infrastructure — from a single interface your team controls from anywhere. Rather than adding layers to your stack, LUCI reduces the variables, hardware, and interfaces your team has to manage. When onsite experience and operational continuity are non-negotiable, LUCI delivers coordinated, real-time execution, end to end.

THE LUCI WORLDVIEW

Complex environments don’t fail because of what they lack. They fail because of what they accumulate.

Every property runs on a stack of vendors, processors, interfaces, and workarounds that each made sense at the time, but create downstream challenges.

LUCI doesn’t address this with an additional tool; it addresses this with the principle of subtraction. By removing variables. Its success is evidenced by the number of things our clients no longer have to think about.

THE OFFERING

LUCI Systems is two forces in one: LUCI, the orchestration platform, and the Systems, the embedded operation.

  • THE PLATFORM

    The software that orchestrates your entire AV environment from a single interface. It runs on standard network infrastructure, connects to any endpoint through open APIs, and puts control of everything in one place — accessible from anywhere.

  • THE SYSTEMS

    The people, processes, and standards that work alongside your team. The same engineers from day one through year five, applying a refined operating standard so the platform gets more capable every year.

MESSAGE PILLARS

Four pillars that anchor every communication. Each stands alone and serves any buyer.

  • 01Complete visibility and control across your property

    One interface surfaces every endpoint, zone, and system across your property. Every team sees the same picture and acts from the same place.

  • 02Align your teams by default

    LUCI connects every team to the same system, so your organization can stop negotiating internally and start executing towards a shared vision.

  • 03Fully activate the guest experience

    Turn passive screens into purposeful moments by planning, programming, and responding to guest signals in real time — turning your A/V infrastructure into a strategic tool for revenue, retention, and brand.

  • 04Invest in the only A/V that gets more valuable over time

    Traditional A/V depreciates and expires. LUCI doesn't. We refine our software, integrations, and operational capability on a predictable annual line item so Year Five is more capable than Year One.

THE SYSTEMS

Six named services. Together, they are the embedded organization that makes the platform work — and keep working — in the real world.

  • 01LUCI FDE — Forward-Deployed Engineering

    Full-stack engineers who embed inside the client's environment — from scoping through continuous refinement. A LUCI FDE is not a consultant, a solutions architect, or a support representative. They work inside the client's environment, build production systems that hold up under real operational conditions, and remain accountable for those systems long after deployment.

    Delivers: one accountable team across the full lifecycle. The engineer who walks the property on day one is the engineer who supports it in year five. LUCI Systems does not hand off engagements.

  • 02Design & Deployment

    In-person scoping, architecture, and standing-up of the platform — by the team that will stay. The FDE team maps the existing environment across IT, AV, operations, marketing, and finance; designs the target architecture against the LUCI standard; consolidates the infrastructure; and stands up the platform, phased so value lands before the full build is finished.

    Delivers: a deployment scoped to the property's reality, executed by the people who will own it — not subcontracted, not handed off.

  • 03Owner's-Rep Partnership

    A standing extension of the client's enterprise — strategic, embedded, accountable. LUCI Systems functions as the client's in-house technology leadership for the guest-facing environment: planning multi-year roadmaps, owning vendor relationships, advising on capital strategy, and representing the property's interests in every technical decision.

    Delivers: senior technology leadership the property doesn't have to build, staff, or replace — present continuously, accountable to the property's goals.

  • 04Training & Enablement

    A standardized curriculum that makes the client's team fluent in LUCI. Training is not an afterthought or a PDF — it is a structured program: role-based instruction, hands-on enablement, and documentation that lives inside the platform. New staff onboard against the same standard; institutional knowledge survives turnover.

    Delivers: a team that runs the platform with confidence, and a property that doesn't lose capability when a key person leaves.

  • 05Support & Operations

    The backbone — the process starts and ends with support. The same FDE team that designed and deployed the platform is the team that answers when something needs attention. No tiered queue. No establishing which vendor owns the incident. One team owns the diagnosis and the resolution, on the property's schedule — including the off-hours when it matters most.

    Delivers: one accountable owner, faster resolution because context is never rebuilt, coverage that matches a property that never closes, and incentives aligned with uptime rather than ticket volume.

  • 06Continuous Refinement

    The discipline that makes the platform appreciate, year over year. Every incident and operational pattern the Systems encounter becomes an input to the next platform release. Problems aren't just fixed at one property — they are engineered out of the platform so no property meets them again. Annually, the embedded team revisits the roadmap: integrations extend, automation sharpens, the standard gets tighter.

    Delivers: a platform that is more capable in year five than the day it was deployed, on the same line item — and a property that inherits every improvement without a migration.

WHAT LUCI IS AND ISN’T

LUCI IS

  • An operating system that connects infrastructure
  • Software, hardware, operating model, and embedded engineering — delivered as one system
  • A platform that appreciates over time
  • A team of seasoned engineers accountable from design through ongoing refinement

LUCI IS NOT

  • A control panel (runs one system at a time)
  • A dashboard (reports on a property, doesn’t operate one)
  • A point solution that creates an integration problem
  • A vendor that hands off after deployment

VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

These rules apply to everything LUCI publishes — website, decks, proposals, outreach, social, video. If a piece of copy doesn’t pass these rules, it doesn’t go out.

Voice rules

Engine and verbs — not layers.

Never use layer as a noun for LUCI (orchestration layer, application layer, middleware layer). Describing accumulation in the client’s stack is fine; naming LUCI itself a layer is not.

Leading choices: orchestration engine (positioning, tagline) and LUCI orchestrates… (headlines, capability copy). Other approved verbs and nouns are fine — runs, operates, integrates, consolidates, refines, platform, infrastructure — when they fit the sentence better.

Watch repetition. If engine or orchestrates appears more than once in a short passage (deck section, page, email), rotate to a precise alternative. Same word three times reads like a crutch, not a voice.

Institutions, not adjectives.

Describe what the platform does for the enterprise. Never describe how the software feels to use. “Operate every endpoint from one interface” passes. “Absurdly simple” does not.

Subtraction over addition.

Lead with what LUCI removes from the environment — variables, vendors, interfaces, refresh cycles — not with the capabilities it adds.

Declarative over promotional.

State facts. Avoid emotional verbs (“revolutionize,” “transform,” “empower”). If a claim needs an adverb to land, it isn’t landing. Cut.

Discretion over display.

No client names in public materials. No percentage claims. Scale and complexity are described in general terms.

Standardization is the advantage.

Never apologize for a standard approach. Standardization is what makes the system reliable. Customization is what broke every A/V environment they’ve had before.

Short sentences. Short paragraphs.

If a claim needs a modifier to land, it isn’t landing. Cut the modifier. Then cut the sentence it was propping up.

Proof & institutional trust

When describing LUCI’s scale, scope, and track record, use language that conveys depth without naming clients or citing specific metrics. These are the approved frames:

SAY THIS

  • Deployed across complex, high-traffic properties
  • Refined through years of operational engagement
  • Built on institutional knowledge accumulated across the portfolio
  • Tested against the demands of 24/7 enterprise environments
  • Trusted by properties that cannot afford failure

NOT THIS

  • Specific client names or logos
  • Percentage reduction or uptime claims
  • Named properties or locations
  • Revenue impact figures
  • Headcount or team size specifics

Approved language

Use these terms consistently across all materials.

USE THESE

  • Orchestration engine, platform, infrastructure
  • LUCI orchestrates, runs, operates, integrates, consolidates, refines
  • Institutional, operational, embedded, accountable, continuous
  • The Playbook, LUCI FDE, the engagement model, the standard
  • Runs, operates, integrates, consolidates, refines
  • Removes variables, shorter list, fewer moving parts

RETIRE THESE

  • Absurdly simple / easy to use / intuitive — consumer register
  • Revolutionize / transform / empower — empty emotional verbs
  • Any percentage reduction or increase claim in brand-level copy
  • Owner’s rep — use LUCI FDE or embedded team
  • Best-in-class / game-changing / conversation-stoppers — pitch-deck language
  • Layer — when naming LUCI (orchestration layer, application layer, “one layer for…”). Use engine or “LUCI orchestrates…” instead
  • Named clients in public materials

By medium

The register shifts by context. The principles never do.

Website & sales collateral — Insightful and precise

Lead with the variable-reduction principle. Name the systems LUCI replaces. State what the platform does for each team. No emotional verbs, no percentage claims. Every sentence should be provable.

Executive pitches & proposals — Authoritative and operational

Open with the problem — accumulation, not lack. Build to the platform as the institutional solution. Let the FDE model and the four commitments close. Speak to the CFO, CIO, and COO simultaneously.

Email & client outreach — Direct and specific

One idea per email. Name the person’s specific problem before naming LUCI. Never open with a feature. Open with a consequence of not having LUCI — a downstream question they’re currently answering that they shouldn’t have to.

Social content — Declarative and confident

Short. Declarative. One idea per post. The worldview is the content — a well-placed sentence from the philosophy section will outperform any product announcement. No hyperbole.

Video & presentations — Conversational but grounded

Anchor to the philosophy before introducing the product. The variable-reduction principle lands well on camera. Use the anchor phrases below. Speak in problems and consequences, not capabilities.
