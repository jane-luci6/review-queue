HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Each persona below describes how a specific buyer experiences AV today, how they enter the deal, where conversations stall, and the message most likely to move them. Lead with the angle most relevant to the person in the room. The underlying platform and engagement model don’t change — only the entry point does.

CEO / GENERAL MANAGER

Owns total property performance — revenue, profitability, guest experience, and brand consistency.

PAIN POINTS

  • AV exists but nobody is running it with intent
  • Promotions don’t execute consistently across the property
  • The environment doesn’t respond to what’s happening on the floor
  • Revenue opportunities pass because the infrastructure wasn’t set up to capture them

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Usually the deal closer, not the initiator. Brought in after a champion has done the groundwork. If things escalate, they’ll sit in on a demo later in the process. In smaller properties, may be both champion and decision-maker.

WHERE DEALS STALL

ROI clarity. They see additional cost for LUCI but don’t see what they can cut because of it. Confusion about the recurring cost model and inaccurate comparisons to traditional AV slow everything down.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Revenue per guest
  • Profitability across the property
  • Consistent guest experience at scale
  • Operational control without complexity

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“Your floor is already influencing guest behavior. LUCI makes that influence more targeted and activating.”

CIO / IT DIRECTOR

Owns system uptime, vendor management, technology strategy, and integration oversight.

PAIN POINTS

  • No unified view across systems
  • Adding or moving endpoints requires specialist knowledge
  • Hardware sprawl taxes the network and IT team
  • Every renewal negotiation happens in isolation

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Often the champion. Feels the operational pain directly and has the technical credibility to evaluate the solution. May initiate or be brought in early to validate feasibility.

WHERE DEALS STALL

Integration and support accountability. They need to know exactly how LUCI sits in the existing architecture and who owns it when something goes wrong.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Reliability and system uptime
  • Reduced specialist dependency
  • Smaller surface area to defend
  • Clear integration path with existing architecture

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“One interface for every system on the property — on standard network infrastructure, without proprietary hardware or specialist programming.”

CFO

Owns capital allocation, operational cost discipline, and long-horizon spend strategy.

PAIN POINTS

  • Racks of proprietary hardware consuming budget and real estate
  • Overlapping vendor contracts with no unified view
  • Recurring refresh costs that reset the clock every five to seven years
  • Maintenance and specialist costs that are unpredictable and hard to forecast

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Rarely an initiator. Brought in to approve or kill a deal already shaped by others. Their approval is required even when their engagement is late.

WHERE DEALS STALL

TCO clarity and contract structure. If the full cost picture isn’t laid out before they enter the conversation, they slow everything down.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Predictable spend
  • Defensible long-term strategy
  • Assets that appreciate instead of depreciating

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“Infrastructure that appreciates. One subscription replaces compounding capital costs — and year five is more capable than year one on the same line item.”

COO / OPERATIONS DIRECTOR / GAMING DIRECTOR

Owns operational efficiency, staff productivity, property-wide performance, and accountability.

PAIN POINTS

  • Fragmented systems create operational risk
  • Staff spend time negotiating between tools instead of running the property
  • When something breaks across systems, no one owns the problem
  • The gaming environment isn’t being used to actively direct guest attention

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Often a strong champion, particularly in gaming-focused properties. Feels the floor-level friction directly. May co-champion with the CIO.

WHERE DEALS STALL

Implementation disruption. They need to know what the transition looks like on a live floor — what goes down, when, and for how long.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Efficiency and reliability
  • Fewer moving parts
  • One accountable team across the full operational stack
  • Gaming environment that drives guest behavior

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“Run a tighter operation. Automate the routine. Respond instantly to the unexpected. One team accountable for the whole outcome.”

CMO / GUEST EXPERIENCE LEADER

Owns guest engagement, in-property spend, brand consistency, and experience design.

PAIN POINTS

  • Fragmented systems make consistent experiences impossible
  • Getting anything on screens requires routing through AV or IT
  • Mistimed or misfired content costs revenue
  • AV failures disrupt the experience at moments that matter most

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Rarely the initiator. Brought in once the conversation has advanced to validate that LUCI solves their execution problems.

WHERE DEALS STALL

Proof of control. They want to see exactly what they can change, how fast, and without IT involvement.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Immersion and brand consistency across every screen and zone
  • Real-time programmability
  • Confidence the infrastructure won’t fail at the wrong moment

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“Deliver the right experience, in the right zone, at exactly the right moment — every time, across the whole property.”

AV / FACILITIES MANAGER

Owns day-to-day AV operation, maintenance, staff training, and incident response.

PAIN POINTS

  • Constant trips to server rooms for changes that should be remote
  • Slow diagnosis across disconnected systems
  • After-hours calls with no clear way to pinpoint the cause
  • Pressure from above to fix issues fast with tools that make it hard

HOW THEY ENTER THE BUYING PROCESS

Almost always involved early — they know the current environment and get pulled in to assess feasibility. Can champion or block depending on whether they feel threatened by the change.

WHERE DEALS STALL

Trust and control. If they feel LUCI displaces their expertise or reduces their authority, they’ll quietly resist. They need to feel like LUCI makes them better at their job, not redundant.

DECISION DRIVERS

  • Remote access and clear system visibility
  • Reliable escalation path
  • Fewer emergencies
  • Retaining expertise and control in the new environment

THE MESSAGE THAT LANDS

“See everything. Control anything. From any device. Without leaving the floor.”
