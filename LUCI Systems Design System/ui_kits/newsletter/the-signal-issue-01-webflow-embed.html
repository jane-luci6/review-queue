In the field

Three days to a future-ready platform

Ameristar Council Bluffs Casino Council Bluffs, Iowa · Penn Entertainment

The challenge

Ameristar Council Bluffs spans a land-based casino and a riverboat. A major land-side buildout and a planned transition off the riverboat were approaching, and with aging equipment from years of incremental upgrades, the property needed a unified, future-ready platform for its next phase of growth.

What LUCI did

LUCI completed a full retrofit in under three days, highlighted by the relocation of all AV processing from the riverboat into the property's IT data center on land — eliminating dedicated AV rooms from the new buildout and reducing future construction costs. The team replaced the head-end systems, amplifiers, and control infrastructure, modernized the system documentation, and completed the work without disrupting daily operations.

The results

LUCI now enables Ameristar to manage all of its complex A/V systems from a single interface — giving every member of the team one place to control the property from anywhere. That foundation supports unlimited operational users on any browser-based device, built-in automation for daily resets and scheduled adjustments, and ongoing enhancements without additional upgrade licensing costs. In practice, that means:

  • One unified view of every endpoint
  • Broader delegation by zone
  • Faster response to daily needs
  • Automated resets and scheduling
  • Simpler maintenance and expansion
  • Lower long-term infrastructure costs

With the system centralized on land, the property is also set up for what comes next: a clean break from the riverboat when the time comes, and a new buildout that plugs into the platform already powering the property — no second install, no parallel system to stand up.

From the playbook

Set a morning reset

One of the most useful things on the platform takes about ten minutes to set up, then runs every day without you.

Here's the idea: build a preset that puts your entire property into a known, intentional state, then schedule it to fire before the floor opens.

What it sets:

  • Right channels on the right screens
  • Volumes calibrated for the time of day
  • Each venue configured the way you designed it

What you get: a property that starts each day the way you planned — not the way it ended up after the last shift. No pit boss finding Judge Judy on the sportsbook screens. No morning walk-through to fix volumes that got knocked around overnight.

To set one up:

  1. Go to Presets and create a new preset with the state you want

  2. Drop it onto the Calendar

  3. Set it to repeat daily at your preferred time

If you'd like help building it out, reach out to your FDE. It's a short conversation and one of the highest-ROI calls you'll make.
